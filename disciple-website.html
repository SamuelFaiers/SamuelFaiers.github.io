Disciple: Website

Disciple is a SaaS product that lets community leaders create their own independent community platform on web, iOS and Android. I was heavily involved in improving the marketing websites user experience.

Background

The sales team had expressed concern that website users who had converted into leads did not actually understand what Disciple was. This meant that sales representatives were spending a considerable amount of time explaining what the product was, instead of selling it.

Solution

I conducted a usability test to understand why users were having difficulty understanding Disciple’s purpose. My findings allowed me to make recommendations on how to improve the user’s ability to see the purpose of Disciple and perceive it as a valuable product.

Outcome

So far, my recommendations have led to a 24% increase in conversions, showing that users better understand the product and its value. The sales team is also spending far less time explaining the product to leads, so they can focus on selling it instead.

Usability test

Due to budgetary and time constraints, the test had to be conducted remotely using a platform called User Brain. I used it to set up the usability test and recruit ten participants from a worldwide pool of testers. I also wrote a “script” for these participants which would guide them through the test in the absence of a facilitator.

Findings

Three out of ten participants successfully demonstrated a good understanding of Disciple, but only after reading through all of the text on the home and “How it works” pages. This showed that at a glance, the website did not successfully convey Disciple’s purpose, requiring users to invest time into reading all the text to gain a good understanding.

“I wish there was more information about it. I would be hesitant to fill in the form because I'm not sure what I'm getting.”

Because it was difficult to find out what Disciple was, the product also did not resonate with several participants as valuable. They expressed that they would be hesitant to subscribe to it because they could not see the benefit of using it over existing platforms.

My recommendations

Bring key visuals out of hiding

On the homepage, key visual information above the fold was hidden within an outdated explainer video that very few users were watching. I proposed to replace this with a visual that immediately shows a real example of a community platform that had been built with Disciple.

A genuine example of what can be done with Disciple would help users visualise the product and therefore understand its purpose. Presenting it as a case study would also act as social proof, persuading them that Disciple is a product with value, that its customers have found success using.

Disciple's makeover

Disciple was also undergoing a large scale rebrand at the same time as this project. This significantly improved the look and feel of the website, representing Disciple as an established, trustworthy and therefore more valuable product to users.

Update product visuals

The product visuals on the homepage were severely out of date and low-quality. Higher quality and up to date visuals would be more engaging to the user. Animating these visuals would also make it easier for users to visualise the product and therefore understand its purpose.

Final tests

To ensure that the changes solved the problem, another usability test was conducted on a prototype of the new homepage. This time, all the participants demonstrated a good understanding of Disciple and easily identified its key benefits.

“It's a tool to build a community. You can modify colours, publish things and do livestreams.”

Launch in progress

Before launching the improvements, they each needed to individually pass one final A/B test. While this would take longer than anyone had desired, this was to ensure that none of them would have a significantly negative impact and make the others look unsuccessful. As of my departure from Disciple, my first recommendation had been launched, resulting in a 24% increase in conversions. I am confident that the other improvements will yield similar results.
