Haberdashery

Haberdashery is a design studio that specialises in creating beautiful works of art using light. My primary roles for this project were creating the site map, designing the UI for mobile and adjusting the UI design throughout the project.

Visit Website

Brief

First, we sat down with our client to determine exactly what they wanted from the website. We ultimately agreed that a minimalistic and elegant website would align best with their brand. It was also made clear that the clients would want to edit the websites content after launch. Therefore, we decided to develop the website using WordPress, allowing the client to edit the content as they see fit in the future.

Sitemap

The primary purpose of the sitemap was to conclude the structure of the website and collaborate with the client to appropriately label each page. The site map also allowed us to determine what content would be dynamic and would therefore need to be developed as a custom post-type on the WordPress backend.

Wireframes

Using Sketch, we created wireframes for each page and post type. I created the first iteration of the mobile layout and adjusted them based on the feedback I received. I also adjusted the desktop layout where necessary.

UI Design

Our clients wished for a minimalist and elegant look and feel to the site. The vast majority of content also consisted of photographs of varying colour schemes, given the variety of pieces Haberdashery create. A clean black and white colour scheme was therefore the most appropriate in order to allow the content to shine through.

Prototype

With the final UI established we exported each screen to InVision and created a prototype for the website. We then showed this prototype to the client who provided feedback that allowed us to make the final adjustments before the development stage. The InVision prototype also allowed the developers to easily acquire the finer details of the UI design such as the font size and weight, making our workflow more efficient.

Post-development

Following the development of the site, the final user tests were conducted by Haberdashery who gave us feedback based on these tests. My role at this stage was to quickly make the final ammendments to the design by adjusting the WordPress templates and stylesheet.
