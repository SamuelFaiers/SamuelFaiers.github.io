LIDC Infographics

  • Data Visualiation
  • Graphic Design
  • Illustration

Throughout November 2017, I was given the opportunity to work two days a week at the London International Development Centre (LIDC) for one month. During my time working there, I was tasked with designing three infographics in order to visualise data provided by LIDC. Given its nature as an academic organisation, LIDC's target audience consists of both students and academics. I therefore considered both groups when designing the infographics that were aimed towards them.

With all the graphics one of my main considerations was colour. I knew that these graphics would be displayed digitally on the web and designed them in RGB colour space. I also chose to use softer colours to ensure that the graphics would not be too straining for both students and older academics.

Geographical Areas of Interest

The first infographic visualises data on the geographical areas of interest of LIDC members. I felt that it was only natural to present the data through a world map, which allows for the geographical regions to be clearly displayed and distinguished by different colours. I then used 'human' icons to display the number of members interested in each region, placing the necessary number of icons in each region. This produced a visually pleasing graphic, which concisely displays the popularity of each geographical region and their density of interested members.

Key disciplines

The second infographic was intended for the LIDC team to understand the key disciplines of LIDC's members. First, with the help of the LIDC team, I sorted the data provided into broader categories such as Medical Sciences or Humanities. Given the target audience, I decided to utilise LIDC's brand identity, specifically the colour and the star that features in LIDC's logo. The data is represented by bubbles that are scaled according to the percentage of members in each category. These bubbles, the largest three specifically, were then laid out using the golden ratio.

Areas of Interest

The final infographic displays data on LIDC member's areas of interest such as agriculture or sustainable development. The illustrations are used to give the audience a clear indication as to what each area means. For example, a wind turbine clearly represents sustainable development because wind is a sustainable source of energy.
